About Me

I'm Anna - a rising junior Computer Science major at Northwestern University, with a focus and passion for education. I'm from a small Northern Californian suburb, Davis, but have grown to love the Windy City (and Evanston too).

At Northwestern, I have been an active member of the Computer Science community. A few friends of mine worked tirelessly with me to help bring a branch of the TechShift Alliance to Northwestern Campus, calling ourselves Develop + Innovate for Social Change. Our mission is to create a platform for students to work at the intersection of technology and social change.

Along with DISC, I am also on the executive board of Women in Computing at Northwestern as Treasurer, after a year of active membership. WiC has been an amazing opportunity to share and teach my passion for technology with young women.

With my academic journey studying computer science, I spend a large portion of my "free" time with hands-on experience in classrooms and working with children. My passion for education has found me in various babysitting, tutoring, and teacher assistant jobs. I work particularly close with Northwestern's Center for Talent Development , working in their offices, weekend programs and summer programs.
